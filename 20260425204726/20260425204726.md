# Become better software developer

How To Become a Senior Developer: 
1. Write code that other people can read, not just code that works. 
2. Understand the business problem before touching the keyboard. 
3. Learn to debug calmly instead of panicking randomly. 
4. Stop measuring yourself only by lines of code. 
5. Take ownership of failures, not just your successes. 
6. Communicate tradeoffs clearly, especially when there is no perfect answer. 
7. Review code with empathy, not ego. 
8. Learn the fundamentals deeply so you do not chase every shiny tool. 
9. Ask better questions before building the wrong thing faster. 
10. Get good at reading existing codebases, because that is the real job most days. 
11. Know databases, networking, caching, and concurrency well enough to not create hidden disasters. 
12. Make systems simpler, not more clever. 
13. Be reliable under pressure, especially during production issues. 
14. Document important decisions so the team does not depend on your memory. 
15. Think in terms of maintenance, scale, cost, and failure modes. 
16. Help juniors grow instead of acting superior to them. 
17. Learn when to move fast and when to slow down. 
18. Build trust by doing small things consistently well. 
19. Speak up when architecture is weak, but back it with reasoning. 
20. Realize seniority is less about syntax and more about judgment.
